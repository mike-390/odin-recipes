Butterhorn Dinner Rolls

TButterhorn dinner rolls date all the way back to colonial times, but I've streamlined the process to make this
recipe easier for new and inexperienced bakers. These classic dinner rolls are light but rich, and slightly sweet.
They're perfect for special occasions or holiday dinners, alongside any main course.

Ingredients

  • 1 (.25 ounce) package active dry yeast
  • ¼ cup warm water
  • ½ cup unsalted butter
  • 1 cup milk
  • 3 tablespoons white sugar
  • 3 ¾ cups all-purpose flour, divided
  • 1 ½ teaspoons kosher salt
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • ¼ cup butter, melted

Directions

  • Step 1:

    Sprinkle yeast onto warm water. Stir gently and let sit until foamy
    and frothy, 15 to 20 minutes.

  • Step 2:

    While the yeast is proofing, place butter in a saucepan with milk
    and white sugar. Turn heat to medium and cook, stirring
    occasionally, until butter melts and sugar dissolves, 3 to 4 minutes.

  • Step 3:

    Remove from the heat and pour into a large mixing bowl; let cool until just very warm to the touch and about 120 degrees F (49
    degrees C), 2 to 3 minutes.

  • Step 4:

    Add 3 cups flour, kosher salt, yeast mixture, and egg; mix gently
    with a spatula until it all comes together and forms a very thick
    and wet batter. Add remaining 3/4 cup flour and mix with your
    fingers, incorporating just the amount of flour you need, until
    dough comes together into a soft, sticky ball.

  • Step 5:

    Cover and let rise until almost doubled in size, about 1 hour.

  • Step 6:

    Transfer dough to a floured surface and knead until smooth and
    elastic, about 5 minutes.

  • Step 7:

    Grease the bowl with butter. Place the dough back into the bowl, cover, and let rise for 30 minutes.

  • Step 8:

    Preheat the oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C). Line a baking
    sheet with a silicone liner (such as Silpat®).

  • Step 9:

    Transfer dough back to a work surface and press it into a 1-inch
    thick circle. Flip over and continue pressing until circle is 1/2-inch
    thick; use a rolling pin if needed to achieve correct thickness. Use a
    pizza tool to cut dough into 12 equal wedges.

  • Step 10:

    Starting at the larger end of one wedge, roll toward the point,
    stretching the ends of the dough out for the first few inches. Pinch
    and press the point into the dough to seal and place, seam-side
    down, on the prepared baking sheet. Repeat with remaining
    dough.

  • Step 11:

    Let rolls sit until they puff up a little, 15 to 20 minutes.

  • Step 12:

    Bake in the center of the preheated oven until cooked through and
    just starting to turn a light golden brown, 20 to 25 minutes.

  • Step 12:

    Remove from the oven and immediately brush hot rolls with
    melted butter. Transfer to a wire rack to cool completely, 15 to 20
    minutes.
